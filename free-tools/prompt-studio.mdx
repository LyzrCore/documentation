## Prompting Techniques

### Chain of Thought Prompting

This technique involves guiding the AI through a step-by-step reasoning process to reach a conclusion. It’s akin to showing your work in a math problem, where each step logically leads to the next, culminating in a solution. Chain of Thought is particularly effective for complex queries that require more than a straightforward answer, enhancing the AI’s ability to generate detailed and reasoned responses.

!["Example of Chain of Thought Prompting"](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:828/format:webp/1*xgEKxuDy-BNn6Efqou4jKg.png)

### ReAct Prompting

ReAct, short for “Read and Act,” is a technique designed to improve the AI’s understanding and response to prompts by incorporating a feedback loop. It involves presenting the AI with a task, analyzing its response, and then refining the prompt based on this analysis to guide the AI towards a more accurate or relevant response. This iterative process fosters a deeper interaction between the user and the AI, resulting in more nuanced and precise outputs.

!["Example of React Prompting"](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:828/format:webp/1*rLUGANxyEf19ITbF9AuaPg.png)

### 5-shot Prompting

This technique leverages examples to teach the AI how to respond to a particular type of prompt. By providing five examples of prompts followed by the desired responses, the AI can “learn” the pattern or the style of response expected. This method is especially useful for tasks requiring a specific format or tone, enabling the AI to generate responses that closely align with the user’s expectations.

!["Example of 5-Shot Prompting"](https://miro.medium.com/v2/resize:fit:828/format:webp/1*6BoBvkCd_T2dgK28zlHTdg.png)

For more information on different prompting techniques, visit [promptingguide.ai](https://promptingguide.ai).
